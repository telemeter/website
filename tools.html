Telemeter loves Observability

You have lots of choices when setting up your team's observability system. We know that none of them are right for every situation and we'll work with you find just the right setup for your needs. Here are some tools we feel are pretty great. We have lots of experience with them and can help you make them purr.

Whatever!

More than any specific tools, we love learning about your needs and even learning new systems. We'll work with your tools of choice if you have them and we'll build a great setup!

StatsD

The most commonly deployed server-side metrics aggregator, it's got client libraries in pretty much every programming language you can think of. It's concepts are widely copied in other implementations. THere are also other, compatible implementations if the original project doesn't fit your operational needs. We also like Datadog's DogStatsD for it's tagging and event extensions.

Metrics

Dropwizard's Metrics library is JVM-specific but it's the quentessential client-side aggregation and metric library. It's often cited as inspiration for similar systems in other languages. It's got great flexbility in outputs and is a pleasure to use.

Datadog

A commercial solution, but offers tons of integrations, powerful toolling and a holistic system for collecting, visualizing, and monitoring your entire infrastructure.

Sentry

Billed "the web's check engine light", Sentry is the boss of error catchers. Usable either hosted or on-premise it has integrations with just about everything.

PagerDuty

The de facto on-call rotation tool, PagerDuty is almost universally used for notifying your on-call employees of problems.
